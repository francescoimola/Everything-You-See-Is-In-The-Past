Atom Chen


"In my recent practice, I try to retain happy moments in memory and represent them in an ambiguous way. This is my friend's pet dog. She is so lovely and she can do anything she wants without getting any blame. I molded the image digitally and transfered it to my canvas, with the impression of her appearance, shadow, and lights."

I shit because I’m a lovely puppy, 2020 


61 x 45.7cm, digitised scan of painting

Atom Chen is a London based independent curator and artist working in installation, sculpture and painting. Examining childhood memories and the effects of nostalgia, his work addresses the disorder of time. Laced with humour, his art warns of a tendency to over embrace the future and the homogenisation of digitised culture. Chen is currently studying MA Curating Contemporary Art at the Royal College of Art and holds a BFA Fine Art from the School of Visual Arts, New York. 

Previous solo and group shows include Counter Point (RCA Courtyard Gallery, London); Seen Forms (HYB4 Galerie, Prague, 2020); Arriving Tomorrow (Harlesden High Street Gallery, London, 2019) Rechewing, RCA (Dyson Gallery, London & China, 2019); SVA Chelsea Gallery (New York, 2019); and DISCONNECT (c.r.e.t.a Roma, Rome, 2018). Atom's work is held in the public collections of the China Health & Medical Development Foundation; Milan Babic Architects; and Tsinghua Holdings AI Industrial Foundation, amongst others.
