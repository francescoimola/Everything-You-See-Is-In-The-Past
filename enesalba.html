Enes Alba


"Climate change fight should be sexy and fun", says Japan’s new environment minister, Shinjiro Koizumi.

This video work by Enes Alba shows raw footage of a "hot guy" taking a shower turning into a selection of mainstream news footages about Australian bushfires that have been collected in just the same day. The news make one immediately alienate from the guy and the eroticism surrounding him.

6 AM RAW FOOTAGE, 2020

3’21”

Elements and sounds of fire and water create an exemplary juxtaposition. The sound of water and fire are quite similar but inspire different "moods". The artist intends to criticize how blazé our lives are: we in our condos, taking long showers under pouring water, while there is a continent burning. In the video, biopolitics meets eroticism; aesthetics meets brutal reality. Ecosystems are falling down while this man takes his morning shower. It is also worth mentioning that it is hard to take a morning shower without having flashbacks of mainstream news: climate crisis penetrates into our lives just as it does into our morning routines.

Enes Alba, born 1997, is a multidisciplinary contemporary artist based in Istanbul. His works focus on queer semiotics, biopolitics, and homoeroticism.
