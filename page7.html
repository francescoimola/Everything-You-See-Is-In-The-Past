Perce Jerrom


Perce Jerrom’s practise explores online cultures to respond to notions of whiteness, toxic masculinity and male fragility. He investigates how these issues have seemingly coincided with a resurgence of right-wing extremism. This is mainly based on research into the indoctrination of young men on forum-based websites such as 4chan and Reddit, but also elsewhere online. 

United, 2019

HD video, 10'

Borne from an initial sociological interest into the freedoms allowed in these often anonymous online spaces, as well as the creative actions partaken by these individuals. These places have become ideological battlegrounds, increasingly events online are having real world implications including recent political upheavals in the West as well as violent attacks committed by individuals with links to these spaces.

His works are based on sourced texts, images and events that have occurred online and offline. Which often reference particular cultural symbols from popular film, tv, music and computer games. Pieces are created physically and digitally, working between video, installation, sculpture and print, mimicking the memetic processes found online. Recent projects refer to these subverted symbols, often placing them in seemingly domestic like spaces. These room-like structures are half built (or partially deconstructed) to allude to the psyche of the possible inhabitants, in relevance to the importance of the home or rooms in psycho analytical dream interpretation.
Elements and sounds of fire and water create an exemplary juxtaposition. The sound of water and fire are quite similar but inspire different "moods". The artist intends to criticize how blazé our lives are: we in our condos, taking long showers under pouring water, while there is a continent burning. In the video, biopolitics meets eroticism; aesthetics meets brutal reality. Ecosystems are falling down while this man takes his morning shower. It is also worth mentioning that it is hard to take a morning shower without having flashbacks of mainstream news: climate crisis penetrates into our lives just as it does into our morning routines.

Perce Jerrom was born in Bristol (UK) in 1985. He graduated from his BA in Fine Art at Oxford Brookes University in 2009, and subsequently attended an unaccredited MA at School Of The Damned in 2015. He recently completed and was awarded a Distinction on the Fine Art MFA Programme at Goldsmiths University.

Working between video, installation, sculpture and print he has recently been investigating toxic masculinity online and its real-world implications. He has exhibited work at Korai Project Space (Cyprus) Gossamer Fog (London), Art-O-Rama (Marseille), Kreuzberg Pavilion (Berlin), Bikini Wax (Mexico City), CBS (Liverpool) and Art Exchange Gallery (Colchester). I have attended residencies at Korai Project Space (Cyprus) Spare Room Residency (Liverpool), Casa Maauad (Mexico City), Lac Esarq (Guadalajara, Mexico) and STCFTHOTS (Denmark). Awards include the John Brookes Memorial Prize, a free studio with Departure Gallery, The Goldsmiths Student Scholarship and the Gilbert Bayes Scholarship.
