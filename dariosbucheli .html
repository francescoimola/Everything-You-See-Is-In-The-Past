Dario S. Bucheli 


The works presented here depict documentary photography of some of Dario S. Bucheli's recent paintings. The work consists of a series of paintings of pictures of paintings, as they are experienced on the internet. They demonstrate a commitment to traditional painterly considerations, and propose a trajectory for addressing contemporary issues relevant to the practice of painting.

Staging Room (CURRENT ISSUE)

The exhibition responds to the increasing number of contemporary artists who share and consume photographs of artwork through social media and other internet platforms. It invites the viewer to reflect on the necessary aspects of perceptual experience that leads to a thorough understanding of a painted image—and the effect that networked technologies have on this process. 

These paintings are now presented within a staging room which is a common feature within commerce-driven websites. This feature acts as a way to propose to the viewer a context in which the art object could be experienced—in turn emphasizing the website’s commercial motivation, and the role that these objects play in the global art economy. 

Staging Room (SURVEY)

Dario S. Bucheli was born in a small industrial city in northern Mexico and is currently based in the United States. He earned his BA in Studio Art, with a concentration in painting from the University of Dallas, and is now an MFA candidate in Studio Art at Texas Christian University. 

He has exhibited work within the United States as well as internationally, and in 2019 he was a participant in the Genius Loci residency in Tuscany. Most recently, he began the 405_Gallery project, a digital gallery space where he acts as director and curator.
